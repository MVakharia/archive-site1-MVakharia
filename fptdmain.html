View source code on GitHub

Gameplay



Just like Sanctum, this game is a first-person shooter/tower defense hybrid.


You'll be placing structures to defend an objective from incoming enemies, but you'll be doing plenty of shooting as well, using your own assortment of firearms.








What sets this game apart from Sanctum is an added twist: real-time maintenance of power sources and coolng mechanisms that keep your turrets going.









Just like your standard gun turrets, they need to be placed and connected up to the towers, and of course upgraded between rounds.









Here's where the 'real-time maintenance' bit comes in: the cooling mechanisms have a pretty short lifespan, which means you'll need to repair them or extend their uptime while fending off enemies.









Thankfully, you'll have the tools to conduct maintenance and repairs from a distance, so it means less traipsing back and forth across the map.









If a cooling system breaks down and isn't repaired in time, the power sources will overheat, shut down and your sentry guns will go offline. Which is bad.









Your job is to fend off enemies while keeping an eye on the cooling systems (marked on your display) to make sure that they don't break.









Enemies will drop scrap metal and ammunition on death, which you can use for maintenance.





Original

The first of two games developed that year, using Unity, UnityScript (Unity's implementation of JavaScript), and Autodesk 3DS MAX.
